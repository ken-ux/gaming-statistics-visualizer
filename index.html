Domain/Data

We are interested in gaming as a domain because it explores a realm of technology that has gained traction and popularity over time, producing complex and interesting advancements in elements such as gaming platforms, companies, and technological innovations (VR, mobile, etc.) Our group has played different video games throughout our lives and it would be interesting to see how our perspective of its evolution parallels trends we uncover. Our merged dataset, sourced here from Trung Hoang and here from Abdulshaheed Alqunber contains information about many games both old and recent! Both authors published their data through Kaggle.

Summary Paragraph

Merging together Hoang and Alqunber’s data, we have information from 55792 game titles. The earliest release date of a game in this dataset goes all the way back to 1975-01-01. In addition, of those game titles there are 20 unique genre or genre combinations and 74 platforms that these games have been played on! And of those platforms, the following are the top 3 with the most games made for it: PC, PS2, DS. In total, the games from this set also brought in 7.1 billion sales globally!

Chart 1: Kenny

This chart was intended to show the relationship between playtime (the hours needed to complete a game) and critic score to find out whether one’s engagement with a game is significant to how those games are rated. For critic score, our merged dataset gives us the choice between using the critic_score values from Alqunber or the metacritic ratings from Hoang. We opted to use metacritic because it shows aggregate review scores on a more nuanced scale (0-100 compared to 1-10).

A scatter plot was used because:

  • Playtime and metacritic score are continuous values which can have large amounts of variability.
  • A non-linear regression could be plotted on top of points to show the general relationship between playtime vs. critic score.

The axes were also adjusted to remove outliers such as individual games with long playtime or trend lines with wide confidence intervals. In this chart, we can see that higher metacritic ratings are correlated with longer playtime. This may inform developers that adding elements to a game which increases playtime through replayability or bonus content may be worthwhile during game development.
